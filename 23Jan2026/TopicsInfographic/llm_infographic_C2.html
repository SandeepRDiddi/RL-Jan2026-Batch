Reward Hacking

Problem:

Reward Model exploited rather than genuinely improved

Example:

If RM rewards length: Model generates verbose, repetitive text to maximize length

Solutions:

‚úì

Human Validation of PPO-generated outputs

‚úì

KL Regularization (higher Œ≤ = stay closer to SFT)

‚úì

Ensemble RMs to reduce single model biases

Distribution Shift

Problem:

RM trained on SFT data; PPO produces increasingly different outputs

Example:

Out-of-distribution samples have unreliable RM scores

Solutions:

‚úì

Active Learning on PPO-generated samples

‚úì

13. Key Takeaways

Why Three Stages Are Essential

  • 1. Pretraining: Statistical foundation. Learn from scale. But no alignment or understanding.
  • 2. SFT: Behavioral shaping. Teach structure + format. But limited by dataset size and quality.
  • 3. RLHF: Nuanced alignment. Learn from preferences. Improve beyond expert demonstrations.

For Pharmaceutical Applications

  • ‚ö° Safety: RLHF learns which warnings matter most to domain experts
  • ‚ö° Accuracy: Pretraining + SFT + RLHF layers ensure correct medical information
  • ‚ö° Productivity: Aligned model focuses on relevant data discovery, saving researcher time

üß† The Bottom Line

Each stage of the training pipeline serves a distinct purpose. Pretraining builds statistical foundations from massive data. SFT teaches structured behavior. RLHF aligns the model with human values and expert preferences. In pharmaceutical applications, this three-stage approach is essential to ensure safety, accuracy, and appropriately calibrated confidence‚Äîultimately serving clinicians and protecting patients.
